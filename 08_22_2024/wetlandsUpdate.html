1. [X] select parks that intersect either a (1) wetlands polygon or (2) wetlands access point

counts/intersections are a little messier than APPROACH #1, because most parks consist of multiple, non-contiguous polygons. I dissolved parks by name/id into the same multipolygon feature before finding wetland intersections (and could alternatively dissolve after finding intersections). however, there are a couple park types (BLM, national monument) that should not be aggregated in this way (i.e., all BLM lands and national monuments in CA have same name/id). depending on the approach we choose, we will need to establish a different method for handling BLM and national monument polygons.

##### parks intersecting wetland polygon or wetland access point
wetlandParks <- parks %>%
  select(suid_nma,
         unit_name,
         county,
         acres,
         geometry) %>%
  mutate(intersects_wetland= case_when(lengths(st_intersects(.,wetlands)) > 0 ~ "park intersects wetland",
                                       lengths(st_intersects(.,wetlands))==0 & lengths(st_intersects(.,access_wetlands_snapped)) > 0 ~ "park intersects wetland access point")) %>%
  filter(!is.na(intersects_wetland)) %>%
  mutate(wetland_park="wetland-park")
